Redesigning Lumosity: Addressing Inequalities

A Case Study

Introduction

Lumosity is a popular brain-training platform designed to improve cognitive skills. However, its existing design may not fully address the needs of racially and socioeconomically diverse users. This project aimed to redesign Lumosity to be more inclusive and equitable, specifically focusing on mitigating racial and income disparities.

Problem

Racial Inequality

Current Lumosity games may lack cultural relevance, disengaging users from diverse backgrounds.

Income Inequality

Membership costs can limit access for low-income individuals.

Solution

Our user-centered redesign focused on these key features:

Free Simplified Reports

This feature addresses income inequality by providing essential progress tracking for all users.

Design Process

Our iterative design process involved user testing and feedback at each stage.

Initial Prototype

We created mid-fidelity prototypes based on initial ideas and identified gaps in the existing Lumosity design.

First Iteration

We made changes based on initial user feedback and input, focusing on improving usability and addressing specific pain points.

Second Iteration

We incorporated insights from readings on UI design principles and learning science, further refining the prototypes.

Final Prototype

The final design reflects a comprehensive understanding of user needs and best practices in UI/UX design.

Outcome

The final prototype incorporates UI design principles to ensure a user-friendly and engaging experience.
